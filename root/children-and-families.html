Children and Families

Modernising Our Services

Daisy separated from her partner on bad terms, and is worried about financially supporting her child alone.

Daisy doesn’t have any savings stashed away, and is limited in the job roles she can seek because of her childcare commitments.

Daisy looks for help online, and discovers the support that is available for people in her circumstances. Informed, Daisy decides to let the DWP know that her child is not receiving financial support from the father, and completes her application within minutes.

We're replacing the old paper-based process with our digital service as well as making it easier to navigate the Child Maintenance journey online for citizens. Guiding more citizens to our digital services will help to reduce the demand on our telephony lines, and free up skilled agents to help those with more complex needs.

We know from experience that some citizens who engage with the Child Maintenance Service may have experienced domestic abuse. We're expanding the online support and guidance we offer to victims. We're also offering more self-help features to aid and inform citizens about their claim, and responsibilities.

The DWP is able to use existing information they hold about Daisy and her ex-partner to confirm their identities. The DWP facilitates contacting the child's father, making an assessment on how he can support his child financially.

When we have difficulty finding someone, external organisations can help us fill in the gaps. We get accurate information about earnings from HMRC, which we can use to calculate the correct amount that Daisy's child should receive.

A faster application process with digital as default means we can process more claims in a shorter amount of time. Applications will be enriched with validated data that make it easier for us to deliver expedient decisions.

The DWP establishes an arrangement with the father that he will make regular payments that will be given to his child.

Parents are informed about everything they need to know to maintain the arrangement, as well as how to report a change in circumstances.

We're not just improving the online claim process, we're making it easier for citizens to manage their claim online. We're also improving how we engage with citizens. We'll use new notification methods, including the ability to communicate digitally.

We help the parents come to an effective arrangement thanks to our robust understanding of customers and their circumstances. We can use the data we've gathered to come to effective, expedient decisions that are fair and measured.

Years pass, and the arrangement that was agreed has seen their child prosper from the monies paid by their father.

Daisy can easily self-manage her and her child’s circumstances through their preferred channel for the lifetime of their arrangement.

We're building features which will aid private Child Maintenance arrangements including communication features to aid in reducing parental conflict. When there have been difficulties, Daisy and her ex-partner have been able to resolve them amicably without breaking their arrangement.
