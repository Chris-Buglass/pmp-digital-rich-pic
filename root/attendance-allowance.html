Attendance Allowance

Modernising Our Services

Aaron is a Retirement Pension claimant who has experienced difficulties with remembering things and making routine decisions recently.

Aaron and his son Matt attend a doctor’s appointment, where they learn that Aaron’s symptoms are associated with dementia, and that they are likely to deterioriate further.

The doctor provides information about what to do next, and where they can find further support. Matt intends to fund his father's care himself.

A cross-governmental approach to signposting will save the citizen's time and effort when engaging with any government service. Our leaflets, notifications and forms will clearly route citizens to access our services.

Matt and Aaron go online to GOV.UK to find what support he might be able to get. They learn that they can get additional support from Attendance Allowance to help with the cost of self-funding.

As Aaron struggles to remember things, it would be difficult for him to manage his own claim. Aaron’s son Matt registers to become his appointee, so that he can easily manage his father’s affairs across DWP services.

We're making it easier to engage with our services, whether it's online, over the telephone, or you're claiming for someone else as a formal appointee. We're designing an online claim process for Attendance Allowance to be inclusive to as many groups as possible.

Now that Matt acts as his father’s appointee, he can apply for Attendance Allowance for his father’s care. Matt fills in the claim online, and registers for notifications to come through to his preferred channel. The DWP instantly receive and begin to process the claim.

Our Identity and Trust technology will make it easier for our staff and services to correctly identify citizens. We'll be able to establish the identity of a citizen against pre-defined confidence levels.

We're simplifying the claim process for citizens and staff by only asking the absolutely neccessary questions we need to determine their entitlement. We're automating the checks for Residency, Presence and other benefits using the shared components the DWP is building for each of these tasks, reducing the need for manual intervention.

We'll automate the simplest case decisions, which will free up colleagues to handle cases with more complex needs. We'll present information to decision makers in a user friendly format, making it easier for them to review claims and deliver a faster decision. We'll improve the agent interface for telephone claims to make it more efficient.

The DWP are able to quickly establish the entitlement to Attendance Allowance, and automatically notify Matt that the claim has been processed and is now in payment.

Streamlined, automated decisions lead to claims being processed faster, ensuring that we pay citizens on time. We'll reduce the need for manually processed payments thanks to our centralised payment validation technology.

We're automating how we issue notifications to citizens following a claim. This will reduce the time that staff spend performing routine clerical processes and shorten the claim process for the citizen.

Matt can easily manage his father's claim online. Fluctuation in costs, payment details and any other change in circumstances can be reported via his preferred channel.

Appointees have to juggle managing someone else’s affairs as well as their own. Self-service can give appointees the flexibility to manage in their own time.

If circumstances change, Matt can easily report it himself, however things don't always go to plan. Sometimes people forget to tell us about changes which can have a serious impact on their claim. If Aaron's care starts to be paid by the Local Authority, we can find out ourselves, and we'll update the claim appropriately. Responding quickly to change reduces the risk of overpaying vulnerable citizens.
